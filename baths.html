About the Roman Baths

The Roman Baths in Bath are a well-preserved historical site dating back to Roman times. The complex includes the Sacred Spring, the Roman Temple, and the bathing facilities used by the Romans. Visitors can explore the ancient architecture, learn about Roman bathing culture, and see the geothermal spring that has attracted people for thousands of years. The museum also displays artifacts from Roman Britain.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Bath, England
  • Coordinates: 51.3813, -2.3590
  • Built: Around 70 AD
  • Type: Roman bathing complex and museum
  • Famous for: Ancient Roman architecture and sacred spring
  • Open to the public?: Yes, daily tours available

Note when going here

The baths can get busy, especially during summer. Visitors are encouraged to wear comfortable shoes for walking around the historic site. Audio guides and exhibitions provide a detailed understanding of the history and engineering behind the baths. Photography may be limited in some areas.
