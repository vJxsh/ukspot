About London Bridge

The name "London Bridge" refers to several historic crossings that have spanned the River Thames between the City of London and Southwark since Roman times. The current bridge opened in 1973 and replaced a 19th-century stone bridge. The medieval bridge before it stood for more than 600 years and even had houses and shops built on top of it.

Quick Facts

  • Location: River Thames, London
  • Coordinates: 51.508056, -0.087778
  • Opened: 1973
