About the London Eye

The London Eye is one of the most popular tourist attractions in London. Located on the south bank of the River Thames, the giant observation wheel offers panoramic views of the city skyline. Opened to the public in 2000, it quickly became one of the most recognizable landmarks in the United Kingdom. Each glass capsule slowly rotates around the wheel, giving visitors a stunning view of famous landmarks across London.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5033, -0.1196
  • Opened: 2000
  • Type: Observation wheel
  • Height: 135 meters (443 ft)
  • Open to the public?: Yes, ticketed attraction

Note when going here

The London Eye provides amazing views of landmarks such as Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament. A full rotation takes about 30 minutes, giving visitors plenty of time to enjoy the scenery and take photos. Visiting near sunset or at night can provide especially beautiful views of the city lights.
