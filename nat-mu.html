About the Natural History Museum

The Natural History Museum in London is one of the world’s leading museums dedicated to natural history, including specimens from plants, animals, fossils, minerals, and more. Founded in 1881, the museum’s iconic Romanesque architecture houses millions of items, such as the famous dinosaur skeletons and the blue whale model. It attracts millions of visitors yearly who come to explore its fascinating collections and interactive exhibitions.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.4967, -0.1764
  • Founded: 1881
  • Type: Natural history museum
  • Famous for: Dinosaur fossils and extensive natural collections
  • Open to the public?: Yes, free general admission

Note when going here

The museum can get busy, especially on weekends and school holidays, so visiting early is recommended. Don’t miss the Hintze Hall with its famous blue whale skeleton and the dinosaur gallery. The museum offers many interactive exhibits suitable for visitors of all ages.
