About Windsor Castle

Windsor Castle is the oldest and largest inhabited castle in the world, serving as one of the official residences of the British monarch. Founded in the 11th century by William the Conqueror, it has been continuously used by successive monarchs for over 900 years. The castle features stunning architecture, beautiful gardens, and the famous State Apartments, which showcase royal history and art collections.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Windsor, England
  • Coordinates: 51.4839, -0.6044
  • Founded: 1070s
  • Type: Royal residence and castle
  • Famous for: Longest inhabited castle, royal ceremonies
  • Open to the public?: Yes, tours and exhibitions available

Note when going here

Visitors can explore the State Apartments, St George’s Chapel, and the castle grounds. Guided tours provide fascinating insights into the castle’s history. Check the schedule for the Changing of the Guard ceremony, a popular event held regularly.
