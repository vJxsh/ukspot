About York Minster

York Minster is one of the largest and most magnificent Gothic cathedrals in Northern Europe. Located in the historic city of York, the cathedral has been an important religious center in England for over a thousand years.

The present building was mostly constructed between the 13th and 15th centuries and is famous for its stunning stained glass windows, including the Great East Window, one of the largest medieval stained glass windows in the world. Visitors can explore the cathedral’s interior, climb the central tower for panoramic views of York, and learn about its history through exhibitions.

Quick Facts

  • Location: York, North Yorkshire, England
  • Coordinates: 53.9624, -1.0819
  • Construction period: 1220 – 1472
  • Type: Gothic cathedral
  • Famous for: Medieval stained glass windows
  • Open to the public?: Yes, visitors and tours available

Note when going here

Visitors should allow time to explore the cathedral’s interior and underground chambers that reveal Roman and medieval remains. Climbing the central tower involves many steps, but it offers excellent views of the historic city of York. Photography is generally allowed, but visitors should respect quiet areas used for worship.
