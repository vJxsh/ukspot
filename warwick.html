About Warwick Castle

Warwick Castle is a famous medieval castle located in the town of Warwick in England. Originally built by William the Conqueror in 1068, it began as a wooden fortress and was later rebuilt in stone. Over the centuries, the castle served as a defensive stronghold and a noble residence. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction where visitors can explore towers, dungeons, gardens, and historical exhibits that bring medieval history to life.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Warwick, England
  • Coordinates: 52.2797, -1.5856
  • Built: 1068
  • Founder: William the Conqueror
  • Type: Medieval castle
  • Open to the public?: Yes, tours and attractions available

Note when going here

Visitors can explore the castle’s impressive towers, ramparts, and historical rooms. Warwick Castle often hosts live shows, medieval reenactments, and family-friendly attractions. Visiting during special events or festivals can make the experience even more exciting.
