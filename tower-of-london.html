About the Tower of London

The Tower of London is one of the most famous historic castles in the world. It was founded in 1066 by William the Conqueror and has served many roles throughout history, including a royal palace, prison, armory, and treasury. Today it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the Crown Jewels of the United Kingdom.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5081, -0.0759
  • Built: 1078 (White Tower construction)
  • Type: Historic castle and fortress
  • Entrance Fee: Paid entry
  • Open to the public?: Yes

Note when going here

Arrive early to avoid large crowds and give yourself enough time to explore the towers, walls, and exhibits. Make sure to visit the Crown Jewels and join a Yeoman Warder (Beefeater) tour to learn more about the tower's fascinating history.
