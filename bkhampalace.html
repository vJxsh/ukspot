About Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the British monarch in London. It has served as the administrative headquarters of the monarchy since 1837 and is currently one of the most famous royal palaces in the world. The palace contains 775 rooms and is used for state occasions, royal ceremonies, and official events.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5014, -0.1419
  • Built: 1703 (expanded in the 19th century)
  • Type: Royal palace
  • Entrance Fee: Paid entry for State Rooms (seasonal)
  • Open to the public?: Yes (during certain months)

Note when going here

Try to visit during the Changing of the Guard ceremony, which usually happens in the morning. Arrive early because it can get very crowded, especially during the summer tourist season.
