About the British Museum

The British Museum is one of the most famous museums in the world, located in London, England. Founded in 1753 and opened to the public in 1759, the museum houses a vast collection of art and artifacts from human history, culture, and civilization. Its collections span over two million years of history and include famous objects such as the Rosetta Stone and the Elgin Marbles. Millions of visitors come each year to explore its galleries and learn about cultures from across the globe.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5194, -0.1270
  • Established: 1753
  • Type: History and art museum
  • Famous for: Global historical artifacts
  • Open to the public?: Yes, free general admission

Note when going here

The museum is very large, so plan your visit in advance if you want to see specific exhibits. Popular attractions include the Egyptian galleries, the Rosetta Stone, and the Great Court with its stunning glass roof. Visiting early in the day can help avoid large crowds.
