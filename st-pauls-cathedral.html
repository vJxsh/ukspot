About St Paul's Cathedral

St Paul's Cathedral is one of the most famous and recognizable landmarks in London. The current cathedral was designed by the architect Sir Christopher Wren and completed in 1710 after the previous cathedral was destroyed in the Great Fire of London. Its massive dome dominates the London skyline and has become an important symbol of the city. The cathedral has hosted many significant national events including royal weddings, funerals, and thanksgiving services.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5138, -0.0984
  • Completed: 1710
  • Type: Anglican cathedral
  • Architect: Sir Christopher Wren
  • Open to the public?: Yes, visitors and tours available

Note when going here

Visitors can climb to the famous dome galleries, including the Whispering Gallery and the Golden Gallery, which offer incredible views of London. The cathedral is also a place of worship, so visitors should be respectful during services. It is recommended to book tickets in advance during busy tourist seasons.
