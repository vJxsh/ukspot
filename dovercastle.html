About Dover Castle

Dover Castle is one of the largest and most important castles in England. Located on the cliffs above the English Channel in Dover, it has played a major role in defending Britain for over 900 years. Because of its strategic location facing continental Europe, it has often been called the “Key to England.”

The castle was greatly expanded in the 12th century by King Henry II, who built the massive Great Tower that still dominates the site today. Beneath the castle are secret wartime tunnels used during World War II, including during the evacuation of Allied soldiers at Dunkirk.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Dover, Kent, England
  • Coordinates: 51.1295, 1.3211
  • Major construction: 12th century (Henry II)
  • Type: Medieval castle and military fortress
  • Famous for: Strategic defense and wartime tunnels
  • Open to the public?: Yes, operated as a historic attraction

Note when going here

Dover Castle is a very large historic site, so visitors should allow several hours to explore the grounds, towers, and underground tunnels. Comfortable walking shoes are recommended due to hills and uneven surfaces. The castle also offers exhibitions, guided tours, and views of the English Channel and the famous White Cliffs of Dover.
