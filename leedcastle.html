About Leeds Castle

Leeds Castle is one of the most beautiful and historic castles in England. Located in Kent and surrounded by a large moat and parkland, it has existed for over 900 years. The castle was originally built in 1119 and later became a royal residence for six medieval queens. Today, Leeds Castle is a popular tourist attraction known for its stunning architecture, gardens, and rich history.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Kent, England
  • Coordinates: 51.2487, 0.6300
  • Founded: 1119
  • Type: Medieval Castle
  • Known as: "The Loveliest Castle in the World"
  • Open to the public?: Yes, with tours and attractions

Note when going here

The best time to visit Leeds Castle is during spring or summer when the gardens and parklands are in full bloom. Visitors can explore the castle interiors, walk around the scenic moat, and enjoy the surrounding attractions such as the maze and falconry displays.
