About Big Ben

Big Ben is one of the most famous landmarks in the United Kingdom. The name "Big Ben" actually refers to the great bell inside the Elizabeth Tower at the Palace of Westminster in London. Completed in 1859, the tower has become an iconic symbol of London and British culture, known for its massive clock and distinctive chimes.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.5007, -0.1246
  • Completed: 1859
  • Type: Clock tower
  • Height: 96 meters (315 ft)
  • Open to the public?: Limited tours available

Note when going here

The best view of Big Ben is from Westminster Bridge or Parliament Square. It is especially beautiful at night when the clock faces are illuminated. Try to visit during the hour to hear the famous chimes.
