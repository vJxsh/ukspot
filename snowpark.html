About Snowdonia National Park

Snowdonia National Park is one of the most beautiful natural areas in Wales and one of the most popular national parks in the United Kingdom. The park is known for its dramatic mountains, scenic lakes, forests, and traditional Welsh villages.

The park is home to Mount Snowdon, the highest mountain in Wales. Many visitors come to hike, climb, cycle, and explore the natural landscape. Snowdonia also contains historic castles, wildlife, and breathtaking viewpoints that attract tourists from around the world.

Quick Facts

  • Location: North Wales, United Kingdom
  • Coordinates: 52.9180, -3.8915
  • Established: 1951
  • Type: National Park
  • Famous for: Mount Snowdon and scenic mountain landscapes
  • Open to the public?: Yes, open year-round

Note when going here

Visitors should check the weather before hiking because conditions in the mountains can change quickly. Wearing proper hiking shoes and bringing water and warm clothing is recommended. Snowdonia offers many trails for different experience levels, and the Snowdon Mountain Railway allows visitors to reach the summit without hiking.
