About Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey is one of the most historic and important churches in the United Kingdom. Located in London, it has been the traditional site for the coronation of British monarchs since 1066. The Gothic-style church has witnessed many royal weddings, funerals, and national ceremonies. It is also famous for being the burial place of many notable figures in British history, including kings, queens, scientists, and writers.

Quick Facts

  • Location: London, England
  • Coordinates: 51.4993, -0.1273
  • Founded: 960 AD (current building begun in 1245)
  • Type: Gothic abbey church
  • Famous for: Royal coronations and historic burials
  • Open to the public?: Yes, visitors and guided tours available

Note when going here

Visitors can explore the impressive Gothic architecture, historic chapels, and the famous Poets’ Corner where many writers are honored. Since it is a popular tourist attraction, visiting early in the day is recommended. Photography may be restricted inside certain areas of the abbey.
